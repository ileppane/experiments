The purpose of this game is to understand stock behavior. The game evolves over several weeks.

Each week, there are customers who demand items from your stock. Each week, you can decide whether or not to make a replenishment order from your supplier.

On the first week you start with some initial level of stock.

Making replenishment orders is costly: you have to pay a fixed cost for each batch that you order.

Holding stock is also costly: you have to pay a variable cost of 40% of the item's value to hold it over the game.

If in some week your stock level is less than demand, you incur backlog that has to be satisfied from your next order.

Backlogs result in extra variable costs, such as reputational damage and goodwill losses.

Your service level indicates how often you have been able to immediately satisfy a customer demand. Service level is under 100% if you have incurred backlogs.
