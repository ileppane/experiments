The purpose of this game is to understand stock behavior. The game evolves over several weeks.

Each week, there are customers who demand items from your stock. Each week, you can decide whether or not to make a replenishment order.

The graph shows your stock level each week. On first week you have an initial level of stock.

Making a replenishment orders is costly: you have to pay a fixed cost for each batch that you order.

Holding stock is also costly: you have to pay a variable cost of 40% of the item's value to hold it over the game.

If in some week your stock level is less than demand, you incur backlog that has to be satisfied from your next order.

Backlogs result in extra costs on top of the standard ordering cost. This is because you will suffer reputation and goodwill losses from stockouts. Backlog ordering costs are 4-times the normal ordering cost.
