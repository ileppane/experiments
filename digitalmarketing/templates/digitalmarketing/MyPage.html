Many companies use social media to market their products. Young consumers especially follow social media accounts of celebrities and influencers and make purchasing decisions based on their recommendations. Promotions of goods and services can be a major source of income for social media influencers.

Description

You are working with a client, an online retailer that sells fashion items. The client wants to increase sales of two of their items: shoes and a handbag.

You have initially approached four different Instagram fashion influencers who have given you their terms of promotion. The IG fashion influencers to choose from are:

  • IG1:
  • IG2:
  • IG3:
  • IG4:

To get their fashion item promoted in an influencer's IG account, the client will have to pay each influencer a fixed fee plus a variable fee that depends on the number of followers of the influencer.

The fixed fee for all influencers is £5,000 to promote the shoes and £3,600 to promote the handbag.

The variable fee is determined by the number of followers divided by 100. In other words, the client will have to pay one pence per each follower that the influencer has.

Example: Assume that an account has currently 181,000 followers. If shoes are promoted in this account, this will cost: [fixed fee £5,000] + [variable fee 181,000 / 100 = £1,810] = [total: £6,810]

Your task

To whom the client should send the handbag and to whom they should send the shoes in order to minimise total cost of this marketing campaign? Note: you cannot send both products to the same influencer.

Your points are the total cost of your marketing campaign, so the lower the points, the better.
