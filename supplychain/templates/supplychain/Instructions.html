Instructions

You play this game in pairs. One is the Retailer and one is the Wholesaler. You do not know who your pair is.

The purpose of the game is to come to an agreement of a supply contract, that consists of a supply schedule over four months (May, June, July, August) and a single wholesale price.

The game starts by the Retailer sending an initial Request for Quotation (RFQ) to the Wholesaler. The Wholesaler then devises an offer that consists of a monthly supply schedule and a wholesale price. The retailer can accept or reject this offer. If accepted, the game ends. If rejected, the Retailer devises a new RFQ. Both players can attach messages to their RFQ's and offers.

The game is active as long as an acceptable agreement is reached. Each time the Retailer rejects the Wholesaler's offer, a new round begins. If the Retailer accepts, the game ends.

Retailer's role

The retailer controls the inventory from which items are sold to end customers. The customers pay a retail price from each item which brings in revenue for the retailer.

To each order in each month is associated a fixed cost per order, that is paid if the monthly order is nonzero. The Retailer also pays the wholesale price for each unit ordered.

The items that are not sold during one month are carried over as retail inventory to the next month. The Retailer must pay a unit holding cost for each item in the inventory. In any given month, the inventory consists of the current month's orders that the Wholesaler has sent, and any leftovers from previous months.

If there are leftovers at the end of August, the Retailer can salvage these and earn an additional profit.

The following parameters are known by the Retailer but not by the Wholesaler: buyer demand per month, cost per order when ordering from the Wholesaler, unit Retailer inventory holding cost, salvage price.

Wholesaler's role

The Wholesaler orders the items from a manufacturer which are then sold to the Retailer. The manufacturer has unlimited capacity to deliver the items.

The Wholesaler must pay a unit price for each item it orders from the manufacturer. It also pays a fixed cost per order if the monthly order is nonzero, and a unit order cost for each item the manufacturer delivers.

The Wholesaler needs to pay an inventory holding cost for the items that it orders from the manufacturer and keeps inventoried before delivering them to the Retailer, but unlike the Retailer, the Wholesaler does not incur inventory between months.

The following parameters are known by the Wholesaler but not by the Retailer: cost per order when ordering from the manufacturer, cost per unit when ordering from the manufacturer, unit Wholesaler inventory holding cost.
