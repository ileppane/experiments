Background

A causal map is a visualisation tool that is typically used to assist managers wishing to explore an issue or take a decision. A causal map consists of several statements representing means or ends, and links that connect the statements together. The direction of a link is always from means to ends. Below is an example of two statements and a link.


increase spending→provide high quality services

You can see from the example above how the link establishes a causal relationship between a mean and an end. At the bottom of the link is the mean, "increase spending", that contributes towards the fulfilment of the end, "provide high quality services". Statements can also be combined to form networks of several statements and links. Below is an example of how one end can be fulfilled by two means.


increase spending→provide high quality services
↑
hire competent people

In other words, providing high quality services can be accomplished either by increasing spending or by hiring competent people. We could also have one mean leading to two different ends, as in the example below.


increase spending→provide high quality services
↓
pay more bonuses

And below is an example of how means and ends can be chained together.


increase spending→expand service capacity→provide high quality services

In this chain of statements, expanding service capacity is an intermediate step to providing high quality.

How causal maps are created

Typically causal maps are created in a workshop that consists of two steps. In the first step the participants are asked to enter statements using post-it notes or networked computers in a brainstorming fashion. In the second step they are asked to create links between the statements created by themselves and others.

While the first step is crucial for the final causal map to be as comprehensive as possible, the second step is likewise crucial to know which ends can actually be accomplished by which means. In our research we are interested in this second step and therefore ask for your help.

Your task

We have created a causal map in a workshop that took place at a university in the midlands region of the UK. The participants in the workshop consisted of doctoral research students and masters students, and their task was to brainstorm issues that the university should focus on in order to provide the students with the best overall academic and life experience.

In the page that follows we will show you parts of the causal map that we created in the workshop. We will then ask you to state whether the means-ends relationships are correct. This is Part 1 of the study. In Part 2 we will ask you to choose between several competing means or ends according to your own personal preference.

After you have completed Part 2, you will move to Part 3 where you will fill a survey that is not related to mapping but that explores your decision making style. After completing Part 3 you are asked to move back to Prolific Academic to receive your reward.
